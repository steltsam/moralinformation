Welcome!

For this research project we are interested to see how people make consumer choices based on limited information. You will be presented with six choice sets. You will be asked to choose between pairs of products, both produced by a different national company. The five most important attributes on which the products differ are presented. The products do not differ on price: they are specifically chosen so that they cost the same.

After having considered these 5 attributes, we ask that you imagine making the decision in real life. Could you for example imagine that you have to replace your old phone and are browsing for a new one? For each choice, click the option that you would then actually choose. Clicking the preferred option initiates the next choice set.

By clicking start the main part of the experiment starts.

Two new pieces of information about the previous products have become potentially available. The outcome of this additional information is of a categorical nature. For example, information about whether a house has a garden can either be 'Yes' or 'No'. On average, both possible outcomes for a piece of additional information are equally likely. This is comparable to presenting two houses from a neighbourhood in which out of all the houses 50% have a garden and 50% have no garden. Sometimes both options have the same outcome and sometimes they have different outcomes - this is also equally likely.

However, the actual outcome of the new information is not freely available. To get this new information, you have to buy it by allocating coins towards the different pieces of additional information. For each choice set you will receive a total of 15 coins which all have to be spent to learn about up to 4 pieces of information. The chance that you will actually get this new information depends on the number of coins you allocate. For each piece of information for each product, you can allocate anywhere between 0 and 10 coins. 0 coins means no chance of finding out the new information, 1 coin means a 10% chance of finding out, and 10 coins means a 100% chance of finding out.

After you have allocated all 15 coins, the 'Find out' button has to be clicked. Depending on your allocation of coins and the random draw the outcome will either be shown or not. After you get this new information, you will have the opportunity to change your initial decision about which product you preferred.

After all your choices have been made, one of those choices will be randomly selected. You will be requested to write an anonymous 100-word paragraph explaining why you chose it, just like you would tell someone. This will only be read by the experimenter.

After all your choices have been made, one of those chosen options will be randomly selected. You will be requested to write an anonymous 100-word paragraph stating why someone else should buy that option, just like a sales representative would. This will only be read by the experimenter.
